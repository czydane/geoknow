Philippine Fault Zone (PFZ)

Type: Left-lateral strike-slip

Notes/Description: This major fault system cuts through much of the archipelago from NW Luzon to SE Mindanao.

Major Trenches (Subduction / Tectonic Trenches)

Philippine Trench

Type: Oceanic trench (subduction)

Approx. Length: 1,320 km

Max / Notable Depth: 10,540 m (Emden Deep)

Notes/Description: Formed by subduction of the Philippine Sea Plate.

East Luzon Trough (Trench)

Type: Oceanic trench

Approx. Length: Not well-defined

Max / Notable Depth: 5,700 m

Located northeast of Luzon; sometimes considered related to the Philippine Trench.

Manila Trench

Type: Oceanic trench

Approx. Length: 1,100 km

Max / Notable Depth: 5,400 m

Notes/Description: Formed by subduction of the Eurasian (Sunda) Plate beneath the Philippine Mobile Belt.

Sulu Trench

Type: Oceanic trench

Approx. Length: N/A

Max / Notable Depth: 5,600 m

Notes/Description: Subduction zone west of Mindanao / Sulu; less frequent large earthquakes.

Negros Trench

Type: Oceanic trench

Approx. Length: N/A

Max / Notable Depth: Unknown depth

Notes/Description: Located west of Negros; depth not well documented.

Cotabato Trench

Type: Oceanic trench

Approx. Length: N/A

Max / Notable Depth: N/A

Notes/Description: Located off southwestern Mindanao; associated with subduction and major earthquakes.
