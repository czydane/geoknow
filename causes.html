Causes & Effects of Earthquakes

Causes

Fault Movements (Tectonic Earthquakes)

  • What happens: Stress builds up along a fault line when tectonic plates move. When stress exceeds the strength of the rocks, it suddenly releases, causing the ground to shake.
  • Common in PH: Philippine Fault Zone, Marikina Valley Fault, and other strike-slip or thrust faults.
  • Example: The 1990 Luzon earthquake (Magnitude 7.7) along the Philippine Fault.

Subduction Zones / Trenches (Megathrust Earthquakes)

  • What happens: One tectonic plate slides beneath another (subduction). The sudden release of accumulated stress along the plate boundary generates powerful earthquakes.
  • Common trenches in PH: Philippine Trench, Manila Trench, Sulu Trench, Cotabato Trench.
  • Example: The 1976 Moro Gulf earthquake (Magnitude 7.9) caused by subduction along the Cotabato Trench.

Volcanic Activity (Volcanic Earthquakes)

  • What happens: Magma movement beneath a volcano can fracture rocks, generating small to moderate earthquakes. Often precedes volcanic eruptions.
  • Common in PH: Mayon Volcano, Taal Volcano, Pinatubo Volcano.
  • Example: Earthquakes before the 1991 Pinatubo eruption warned scientists of the impending eruption.

Other Causes (Secondary / Less Common)

  • Human-induced: Mining, reservoir-induced seismicity (large dams), underground explosions.
  • Landslides or rock collapse may produce minor localized quakes.

Effects

Primary Effects

  • Ground shaking: Causes buildings to collapse, roads to crack, bridges to fail.
  • Surface rupture: Fault lines may visibly split the ground.
  • Tsunamis: Megathrust earthquakes in trenches can displace seawater, causing deadly waves (e.g., 1976 Moro Gulf tsunami).
  • Volcanic eruptions: Earthquakes can trigger volcanic eruptions if they destabilize magma chambers.

Secondary Effects

  • Landslides: Especially in hilly or mountainous regions (e.g., Cordillera, Bicol).
  • Flooding: Can occur if rivers or dams are damaged.
  • Fires: Broken gas lines or electrical wires may ignite fires after earthquakes.
  • Economic loss: Damage to homes, infrastructure, agriculture, and businesses.
  • Casualties: Injuries and loss of life due to building collapse, tsunamis, landslides, and volcanic eruptions.

Specific Philippine Context

Cause Types of Effects in PH
