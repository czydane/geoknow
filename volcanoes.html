Volcanoes in the Philippines

Mayon Volcano

Height: 2,463 m
Type: Stratovolcano
Location: Albay, Bicol
History: Over 50 eruptions (last notable: 2018, 2023).

Description: Known for its perfect cone shape; one of the most active volcanoes in the Philippines. Mayon is the archetype of a symmetrical stratovolcano and one of the world most active ones. It has frequent eruptions producing pyroclastic flows, mud flows and ash falls that repeatedly triggered large-scale evacuations. Mayon's most violent eruption, in 1814, killed more than 1200 people and devastated several towns. The volcano rises 2462 m above the Albay Gulf and has very steep upper slopes averaging 35-40 degrees capped by a small summit crater. Historical records of eruptions date back to 1616 and range from strombolian to basaltic plinian, with cyclical activity beginning with basaltic eruptions, followed by longer term andesitic lava flows.

Taal Volcano

Height: 311 m
Type: Complex volcano
Location: Batangas
History: 38 recorded eruptions; major 2020 eruption.

Description: Located on an island within a lake; one of the world‚Äôs smallest active volcanoes. The Taal caldera is largely filled by Lake Taal, whose 267 sq km surface lies only 3 m above sea level. The maximum depth of the lake is 160 m, and contains several eruptive centers submerged beneath the lake. All historic eruptions took place from the 5-km-wide volcanic island in the northern-central part of the lake. The island is formed by overlapping stratovolcanoes, cinder cones and tuff rings (maars). Historic eruptions have seen the constant change and growth of the island. Taal caused one of the worst volcano disasters in history: its eruption in 1911 killed 1334 people and caused ash fall as far as Manila city.

Kanlaon Volcano

Height: 2,435 m
Type: Stratovolcano
Location: Negros
History: Frequent mild eruptions; activity 2020‚Äì2023.

Description: Highest peak in the Visayas; forested and protected. Canlaon, part of the Negros Volcanic Belt, is a large stratovolcano dotted with numerous flank cones and craters, many of which contain lakes.The summit of Kanlaon contains a 2-km-wide, elongated caldera with two craters. One is inactive and contains a crater lake. The second crater to the south is smaller, higher and contains the historically active vent, Lugud crater. Lugud crater is 250 m wide and 150-200 m deep. The base of Kanlaon measures an area of 30 km x 14 km. Historical eruptions from Kanlaon have been recorded since 1866.

Bulusan Volcano

Height: 1,565 m
Type: Stratovolcano
Location: Sorsogon
History: Explosive ash eruptions (1918‚Äì2022).

Description: Known for sudden steam-driven (phreatic) eruptions.Bulusan is a young andesitic volcano built upon the NE rim of an older dacitic-to-rhyolitic caldera, the 11-km wide Irosin caldera, which was formed about 35,000-40,000 years ago. Bulusan is flanked by several other large intracaldera lava domes and cones, including the prominent Mount Jormajan lava dome on the SW flank and Sharp Peak to the NE. The summit of 1565-m-high Bulusan volcano is unvegetated and contains a 300-m-wide, 50-m-deep crater. Three small craters are located on the SE flank.Many moderate explosive eruptions have been recorded at Bulusan since the mid-19th century.

Hibok-Hibok Volcano

Height: 1,332 m
Type: Stratovolcano
Location: Camiguin
History: Deadly eruption in 1951.

Description: Part of the Pacific Ring of Fire; island known as ‚ÄúIsland Born of Fire.‚Äù Hibok-Hibok volcano (also known as Catarman volcano) is the youngest and the only historically active volcano on Camiguin Island, which is located 9 km off the north coast of Mindanao Island, Philippines. Camiguin island itself is a 292 sq km oblate, 20 km long island composed of 4 overlapping stratovolcanoes and some flank cones. Young Hibok-Hibok lies in the NW of the island about 6 km NW of Mt. Mambajao. It is andesitic-to-rhyolitic in composition and contains several lava domes, including Mt. Vulcan on its NW flank. Major eruptions during 1871-75 and 1948-53 formed flank lava domes and produced pyroclastic flows that devastated villages at the coast.

Musuan Peak (Calayo)

Height: 646 m
Type: Lava dome
Location: Bukidnon
History: Minor eruptions in the 1800s.

Description: Gently sloping dome covered with grasslands. Musuan volcano (also known as Calayo volcano) is an isolated lava dome near the Pulangi River at the western base of Mt Tangkulan (1678 m) in Bukidnon Province in central Mindanao, Philippines. The low cone is grass-covered and andesitic-to-dacitic in composition. Musuan last erupted in Dec 1886, as a Jesuit priest reported who visited the volcano 4 years later in 1891, at which time there was still strong fumarolic activity, which prevented closer examination. The eruption was probably phreatic in nature, and was reported to have "burned everything around it".

Parker Volcano (Mt. Melibengoy)

Height: 1,824 m
Type: Stratovolcano with crater lake
Location: South Cotabato
History: Last eruption recorded 1641.

Description: Home to Lake Maughan (crater lake). Parker volcano (also known as Falen locally) is a low, but steep-sided vegetated stratovolcano located 30 km west of General Santos City, near the southern tip of Mindanao Island, Philippines. The andesitic-dacitic volcano contains a 2.9 km diameter summit caldera with a lake, Lake Maughan (2 km diameter). Parker was recently identified as the source of a major explosive eruption in 1641, which caused devastating pyroclastic flows and lahars, and heavy ash fall and darkness over Mindanao Island. The 1641 eruption was one of 3 large explosions of Parker volcano within the past 3800 years. It created the present-day summit caldera.

Smith Volcano

Height: 688 m
Type: Stratovolcano
Location: Babuyan Islands, Batanes
History: Eruptions in 1924 and 1929.

Description: One of the active volcanoes in the Babuyan group. Smith volcano (Mt. Babuyan) 4 km to the NW of Babuyan Claro is the youngest volcano on the island and forms a beautiful 668 m high symmetrical basaltic-andesite cinder cone with ca. 3 cubic km volume. Both Babuyan Claro and Smith volcanoes have apparently been active in historical time, although it is not always certain which volcano erupted. Eruptions at Babuyan Claro are typically strombolian and/or phreatomagmatic. The Askedna Hot Spring is located at the southern base of Babuyan Claro.

Banahaw Volcano

Height: 2,158 m
Type: Stratovolcano
Location: Quezon & Laguna
History: Last recorded eruption 1730.

Description: Sacred mountain; popular hiking site with springs and forests. Banahaw has a 2-km-wide, 600-m-deep summit crater open to the SSW and contained a crater lake until 1730, when it drained to form mudflows. Similar events took place in 1743,1843 and 1909.A large collapse of the NE flank of Banahaw produced a debris avalanche deposit with a volume of 5 cubic km. The event lest a 8 km wide and more than 1 km deep horse-shoe shaped depression extending from 1700 m to 650 m altitude.The oldest deposits from Banahaw volcano are known as the Lucena pyroclastic flow deposits, visible on the shore at Tayabas Bay near Lucerna City and at the eastern base of Banhao de Lucban stratovolcano.

Mount Arayat

Height: 1,026 m
Type: Stratovolcano
Location: Pampanga
History: No recorded eruptions.

Description: Lone mountain rising from the plains; popular hiking site. Mount Arayat is a dormant stratovolcano in Pampanga, Philippines, standing at 1,026 meters and surrounded by plains. It has two peaks, features a breached crater from an ancient debris avalanche, and is known for its lush forests, hiking trails, and cultural significance as the home of local legends like the goddess Maria Sinukuan. Despite being dormant, it has weak fumarolic activity at its northwest summit. There are no records of historical eruptions, but weak fumarolic (steam) activity is present on the northwest summit. It was declared a national park in 1933 and is a popular tourist destination and central to local folklore, notably the legend of Maria Sinukuan, a goddess said to reside on the mountain, often associated with the "White Rock" lava dome. The mountain's diverse plant life supports vital ecological functions like water filtration and soil conservation for surrounding communities also a popular destination for hikers, with trails ranging from beginner-friendly to challengin.It offers scenic views of the surrounding plains and is a prominent landmark in the region.

Mount Apo

Height: 2,954 m
Type: Stratovolcano (potentially active)
Location: Davao Region
History: No confirmed eruption; fumaroles present.

Description: Highest peak in the Philippines; rich biodiversity. Mount Apo is the highest mountain in the Philippines, also a dormant stratovolcano on the island of Mindanao known for its high biodiversity and scenic beauty. It reaches an elevation of 2,954meters (9,692 feet) and is a protected area with diverse flora and fauna, including the Philippine Eagle. The mountain is popular for hiking and features attractions like hot springs, lakes, and waterfalls. A protected area known as Mt. Apo Natural Park, which is also an ASEAN Heritage Park.Includes Lake Agco, Lake Venado, and numerous waterfalls like Tudaya and Mabbu, as well as hot springs. Home to a vast array of plant and animal species, considered a center of endemism in Mindanao. The Philippine eagle (the national bird) and the Apo sunbird are just two of the species found here. The mountain is considered a sacred place by several indigenous groups who live on its lower slopes. Includes various temples and cultural sites like the To temples.

It is a protected area called the Makiling Forest Reserve, managed by the University of the Philippines Los Ba√±os (UPLB) and renowned for its rich biodiversity and geothermal features like hot springs. Mount Isarog

Height: 2,000 m
Type: Stratovolcano
Location: Camarines Sur
History: No recorded eruptions.

Description: Biodiversity hotspot; home to endemic species. Mount Isarog is a dormant stratovolcano in Camarines Sur, Philippines, with a peak of about 1,966 meters. It is part of the Bicol Volcanic Arc and is known for its rich biodiversity, which is protected within the Mount Isarog National Park. The mountain is a key watershed for the region, featuring lush forests, waterfalls, and hot springs, and is popular for eco-tourism and hiking. The park, which covers a large portion of the mountain, is a globally recognized Key Biodiversity Area.The mountain's vegetation includes various forest types, from lowland forest to montane and mossy forests at higher elevations. It is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including endemic species like the Isarog shrew-mouse. Many threatened and restricted-range bird species have also been recorded here. It is a vital watershed for surrounding communities. Hiking to the summit, visiting waterfalls like Malabsay Falls and Nabuntulan Falls, and exploring hot springs are popular activities.

Mount Kalatungan

Height: 2,860 m
Type: Extinct volcano / mountain
Location: Bukidnon

Description: Part of the Kitanglad Mountain Range. Mount Kalatungan is a potentially active stratovolcano in Bukidnon, Northern Mindanao, Philippines, standing at approximately 2,880 meters (9,450 feet). It is the fifth-highest mountain in the Philippines and is known for its challenging, non-technical trails that traverse diverse forest types, from lowland to a mossy upper-montane forest. The mountain is considered sacred by the indigenous Manobo and Talaandig peoples, who call it Keretungan, and is part of the Kalatungan Mountain Range Natural Park. It is part of a protected area that is rich in biodiversity, with a high number of endemic and endangered species of plants, including orchids, and vertebrates, such as the Philippine eagle. The Manobo and Talaandig indigenous peoples consider Mount Kalatungan sacred and refer to it as "Keretungan".
